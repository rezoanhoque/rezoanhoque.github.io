Mohammad Rezoanul Hoque

I'm a Ph.D. student in Financial Economics at the University of New Orleans, concurrently pursuing an M.S. in Computer Science. My research spans financial economics, machine learning, and applied data science — with particular focus on reinforcement learning in financial decision-making, wealth inequality, and green industrial policy.

Previously, I've worked with the USDA, World Bank, Asian Development Bank, and Bangladesh Institute of Development Studies. I was a 1st Prize Winner at the USDA AMS-AAEA Data Visualization Challenge and an invited expert speaker at the U.S. State Department Foreign Service Institute.
